# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

**Primary audience (this round)**: ~100 senior CHROs and Chief People Officers attending the Working Futures CHRO event in Barcelona, June 10–11, 2026.

**Viewing context**: a 1-hour facilitated working session. The site is projected onto a room screen and driven by OXYGY facilitators (Joseph Thomas, Edoardo Monopoli, Yuji Develle) using arrow keys. Audience reads at distance, often in dim conference lighting. They never touch the site themselves.

**Disposition**: senior, time-poor, sceptical of vendor pitches. Pattern-matched against thousands of generic AI tool decks. They are looking for substance, point-of-view, and signs that the people in front of them have thought harder than the average consultancy.

**Job to be done**: orient quickly to OXYGY's specific value in the Strategy + Data space, build trust in the facilitators, leave with one or two strong points of view sharp enough to justify booking a follow-up conversation.

**Future audiences (later rounds, not in scope this round)**: pre-event teaser viewers and post-event leave-behind viewers — same demographic, different viewing context (self-paced on personal devices). The PRODUCT.md will be updated when those surfaces are scoped.

## Product Purpose

A presenter-driven stage surface (`/day-1`) used by OXYGY facilitators to deliver a Day 1 working session on Strategy + Data at the Barcelona CHRO event. The site frames OXYGY's offering — Leadership Strategic Agenda + Data Foundation Mapping — and supports (rather than replaces) facilitated discussion.

The site exists to solve a specific failure mode from the previous Working Futures event (Bird & Bird): the workshop was successful as theatre, but attendees "evaporated" afterwards because the call-to-action was weak and the OXYGY value proposition didn't crystallise. This site is the chassis that makes the value proposition land hard enough to convert.

Success looks like: senior CHROs leave the room with two or three sharp OXYGY positions they can recite. They book follow-up conversations. They forward a link to a peer.

## Brand Personality

Three words: **Considered. Direct. Generous.**

- **Considered** — every word and surface choice deliberate. No filler. The opposite of the consulting-deck disease where every slide hedges and every framework is an acronym.
- **Direct** — takes a real point of view. *"Strategy without data is fantasy. Data without strategy is noise."* Not *"we recommend a balanced, multi-stakeholder approach."*
- **Generous** — respects the audience's intelligence and time. Lets ideas breathe. Doesn't fill every pixel with text. Allows silence on stage.

Voice: senior peer-to-peer. The facilitator and the audience are equals in the same problem space. Not vendor-to-buyer. Not faculty-to-student.

## Anti-references

- **Generic AI tool marketing** — purple gradients, glassmorphism, vague "transform your business with AI" copy, generic stock illustrations of robotic hands, dark-mode with neon accents.
- **Consulting-deck disease** — 47 bullets per slide, every claim hedged, every framework an acronym, McKinsey-style minimal-but-anodyne layouts, blue-grey-everything palettes.
- **Editorial-magazine aesthetic** — display serif + italic + ruled separators + small-caps section labels (Quanta Magazine / The Atlantic / 2024-era Stripe blog). Currently a saturated lane per the Impeccable skill's reflex-reject list. Avoid the reflex.
- **Conference-keynote tropes** — animated checkmarks, scaling pyramid graphics, abstract network-node diagrams, all-caps tracking on every section heading.
- **The previous Bird & Bird workshop format** — four content-heavy stations, no clear call-to-action, attendees impressed in the moment but unconvertible afterwards. The new surface has to crystallise a sale, not just stage a workshop.

## Design Principles

1. **Discussion is the product. Content is the chassis.** Every frame leaves room for the facilitator to talk. If a frame can't be summarised in 5 seconds while reading, it's too dense. The chassis sets up the conversation, not the other way around.

2. **One idea per frame, one accent per frame.** No frame carries multiple competing points. No frame uses more than one soft accent colour (pale yellow OR lavender OR peach, never two together). The discipline produces clarity that lands in a senior room.

3. **Production-grade silence.** Empty space is a design choice, not a missing element. A frame with three sentences and a single teal underline reads more confident than one with twelve cards and a checklist.

4. **Take a real position.** OXYGY says something specific. The anti-references include consulting hedge language. Pick the line and own it: *"Your job isn't to build the data layer. Your job is to demand it."* If the line is not memorable enough to be quoted back at us, it's not strong enough.

5. **Distance-readable by default.** Every type and contrast decision optimises for the back row of a projected conference room. Body type at 20px floor, display type clamp 60–96px, contrast ratios push past AA toward 7:1 where the aesthetic permits.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- **WCAG AA conformance** throughout. Push to AAA on contrast where it doesn't fight the aesthetic — the projection context demands extra contrast headroom regardless of formal conformance.
- **Projection-optimised**: minimum body type 20px, display type clamp 60–96px. Target body-on-background contrast 7:1, display-on-background 10:1.
- **Reduced motion**: respect `prefers-reduced-motion`. The single staggered reveal on frame entry must short-circuit to instant.
- **Keyboard navigation**: all stage controls reachable by keyboard (per the design spec — arrow keys, Esc, T).
- **Colour-blind safe**: soft accents (pale yellow, lavender, peach) carry positional and label meaning, never colour-only meaning. Critical information never relies on hue alone.
- **Internationalisation**: not required this round (English-only, single live event).
